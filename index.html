Steve Jobs: A Timeline

"Innovation distinguishes between a leader and a follower."
- Steve Jobs

  • February 24, 1955 Steve Jobs was born to Abdulfattah Jandali and Joanne Schieble. He was adopted as a young infant by Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • 1960 Jobs Family Moved to Mountain View.
  • 1968 Steve Jobs got his first job working at Hewlett-Packard.
  • 1969 Met Steve Wozniak.
  • 1972 Jobs and Wozniak build and sell illegal blue boxes, allowing users to make telephone calls for free.
  • 1973 Jobs enrolled at Reed College before dropping out after a semester and taking a job working for Atari.
  • 1976 Wozniak and Jobs show the first board for the Apple I.
  • 1977 After beginning in Jobs' garage, Jobs shows the Apple II at the West Coast Computer Fair.
  • 1980 Apple Goes Public making Jobs who is already successful, remarkably wealthy.
  • 1984 Macintosh Introduced.
  • 1985 After a coup at Apple that stripped Jobs of executive responsibility, he, along with some other individuals from Apple, founded NeXT.
  • 1986 Jobs purchased the computer animation division of George Lucas' ILM and incorporates it as Pixar.
  • 1989 Jobs named Entrepreneur of the Decade.
  • 1991 Pixar Signed Deal with Disney.
  • 1996 Apple Purchased NeXT and brought Jobs back onboard as an advisor.
  • 1997 He returns to his former role as CEO of Apple.
  • 2001 Release of the first iPod.
  • 2003 Jobs diagnosed with Pancreatic Cancer.
  • 2007 Jobs introduced the item most closely associated with Apple today: the iPhone. The iPhone 3G followed in 2008.
  • 2010 Release of iPad.
  • 2011, Jobs resigned from Apple, handing the reigns to Tim Cook after his health had worsened.
  • October 5, 2011 Steve Jobs died at home on October 5, 2011, surrounded by his family.
